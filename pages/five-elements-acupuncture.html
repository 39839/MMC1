Dr. Min Lu, MD

Licensed Acupuncturist & Herbalist

Integrative Medicine Practitioner

Dr. Min Lu is a licensed acupuncturist and Chinese medicine specialist providing holistic health care to people of all ages throughout Gaithersburg, Maryland, and Montgomery County.

She earned her master's degree from the Virginia University of Integrative Medicine in Vienna, Virginia, where she received extensive training in functional health care and traditional Chinese medicine.

Dr. Lu studied under Prof. Wang Biqin, a renowned TCM gynecologist, and is a member of Prof. Guo Zhiqiang's National Inheritance Studio, specializing in women's health including infertility, ovarian dysfunction, polycystic ovary syndrome, menstrual irregularities, menopausal syndrome, pelvic inflammatory disease, and postnatal care.

Inspired by witnessing athletes benefit from minimally invasive treatments like acupuncture and massage during the 2008 Beijing Olympic Games, Dr. Lu dedicated herself to helping others achieve wellness through traditional Chinese medicine.

By combining Eastern medicine wisdom with training in anatomy and physiology, Dr. Lu helps patients achieve active, healthy, and pain-free lives. At Five Elements Acupuncture & Wellness, she provides a wide range of services including acupuncture, cupping therapy, moxibustion, massage therapy, and stress management for conditions such as depression, insomnia, allergies, and chronic pain.

Preparing for Your Acupuncture Treatment

Simple habits before and after each acupuncture appointment help you feel relaxed, supported, and ready to embrace the healing process Dr. Lu designs for you.

Before Your Session

  • Enjoy a light, nourishing meal and arrive hydrated to support acupuncture circulation.
  • Wear comfortable clothing that can easily be rolled to access acupuncture points on the arms and legs.

During Treatment

  • Settle into a quiet, spa-like environment while Dr. Lu places acupuncture needles with precision and care.
  • Experience complementary therapies such as cupping therapy, moxibustion, or massage therapy when appropriate.

Aftercare Tips

  • Drink water, rest, and notice how your body responds as the acupuncture session continues to work.
  • Follow the customized acupuncture care plan, including herbal medicine and gentle exercises between visits.
